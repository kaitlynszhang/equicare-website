Our Foundational Research

AI-Powered Equine Health Assessment: A Multi-Modal Approach

Our Research Team | 2024

Abstract

This study integrates machine learning models and large language models (LLMs) to analyze equine health data, aiming to predict horses' psychological states and identify key influencing factors. Using a comprehensive dataset containing 2,057 horse samples across 28 attributes, we applied decision tree and K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) algorithms to predict health outcomes. The results show exceptional accuracy: 95%+ for general health assessment and behavioral analysis, with 80% accuracy specifically for surgical outcome predictions.

Key Findings

95%+
Health Assessment Accuracy

Our machine learning models successfully predict equine health outcomes and behavioral states with exceptional reliability.

2,057
Horse Samples Analyzed

Comprehensive dataset covering 28 physiological and behavioral attributes.

80%
Surgical Outcome Prediction

Specialized accuracy for predicting surgical risks and post-operative outcomes in equine patients.

Clinical Significance

  • Heart Rate Correlation: Strong predictor of psychological and health conditions
  • Movement Patterns: Key indicator of equine well-being and stress levels
  • Blood Parameters: Critical markers for early health intervention
  • Human-Horse Psychology: Rider psychological states significantly influence horse behavior and performance

Methodology

Our research employed a multi-modal approach combining:

  • Decision tree algorithms for interpretable health classification
  • K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) for pattern recognition in physiological data
  • Large Language Models for behavioral analysis and psychological state assessment
  • Correlation analysis across 28 physiological and behavioral attributes
